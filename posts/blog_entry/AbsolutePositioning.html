Absolute Positioning

This page is to show you a little bit about absolute positioning. This is one of the ways to work with the layout of your site via CSS. It can get tricky though to understand absolute vs. relative and the relationship of a child and it's parent. Below I shared some examples of how different settings can impact the positioning of the boxes.

Some examples:

Relative box will always remain aligned to the left side of the page while absolute is 10px to the left of its parent relative box. Relative is happily sitting on the bottom border of its section as it has no veritical positioning. Absolute box will always begin under last line of text in relative box as it has no veritical positioning in this case.
